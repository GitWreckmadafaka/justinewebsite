About me

Hello, my name is Justine Edrian Umerez, and I am passionate about all things related to information technology. As a tech enthusiast, I am constantly exploring new technologies, learning about the latest advancements, and applying my knowledge to various projects. My expertise in IT encompasses a broad range of topics, including programming, networking, cybersecurity, and database management. Whether it's designing innovative solutions, troubleshooting complex technical issues, or collaborating with teams to achieve project goals, I am always ready to take on new challenges and make a positive impact in the field of information technology.

PERSONAL SKILLS

Basic Web Development

As an IT student, I gained hands-on experience building simple websites and systems using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and PHP. In several school projects, we developed responsive web pages, contact forms, and login systems. I also became familiar with frameworks like Bootstrap for designing user-friendly interfaces. Although I'm still improving, I can already create basic dynamic websites and link them to a database using PHP and MySQL.

Database Management

Our curriculum covered relational databases, particularly MySQL. I learned how to create, modify, and manage databases using SQL commands. I applied these skills in group projects, such as a student record system and a simple inventory application. I also understand how to connect databases to a backend, perform CRUD operations, and ensure basic data integrity and security. It's one of the most important skills I developed since almost every system uses data storage.

Problem-Solving and Debugging

Through programming subjects and projects, I learned how to approach problems logically and identify issues in code. I often use debugging tools and console outputs to trace bugs and fix them step-by-step. Whether it's a syntax error or a logical mistake, I learned not to panic but to stay patient and analyze the code. This skill is useful not only in coding but also in general tasks like troubleshooting software issues or improving system performance.

Version Control with Git and GitHub

Collaborating on group projects taught me the importance of version control. I learned how to use Git to manage code versions and prevent conflicts when multiple people are working on the same project. I also became familiar with GitHub, where we uploaded our code repositories for easy collaboration and submission. Although we mostly used basic commands like commit, push, and pull, it helped us work more efficiently as a team.

Networking Fundamentals

We had practical and theoretical lessons on computer networks, where I learned about IP addressing, DNS, subnetting, and different network topologies. We also did lab activities where we set up LAN connections and configured routers using simulation tools like Cisco Packet Tracer. This helped me understand how data travels across networks and how devices communicate securely and efficiently.

Cybersecurity Awareness

In some of our subjects, especially those related to web development and systems administration, we were introduced to basic cybersecurity practices. I learned how to protect web forms from SQL injection using prepared statements and how to secure sessions to prevent unauthorized access. We also discussed topics like strong password policies, data privacy (especially under the Data Privacy Act of 2012), and the importance of keeping software updated. These practices became part of our habit when working on any IT-related project.

A BLOG ABOUT INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY

Information technology

July 26 , 2023

Hello and welcome to my blog post on the topic of information technology! As a first-year student, I am excited to share with you what I have learned so far about this ever-evolving field.

Information technology, or IT for short, refers to the use of computers, software, and telecommunications equipment to store, process, and transmit data. It has become an essential part of our daily lives, from the devices we use to communicate with each other to the software that helps us manage our finances and stay organized.

One of the most fascinating aspects of IT is its rapid pace of innovation. New technologies are constantly emerging, and old ones are constantly being replaced. For example, just a few years ago, flip phones were the norm, but now we all carry smartphones with powerful processors and advanced cameras.

In my opinion, one of the most exciting areas of IT right now is artificial intelligence (AI). AI refers to the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that usually require human intelligence, such as visual perception, speech recognition, decision-making, and language translation. AI has the potential to revolutionize many industries, from healthcare to finance to education.

Another area of IT that I find interesting is cybersecurity. As we increasingly rely on technology to store and transmit sensitive information, it becomes more important than ever to protect that information from cyberattacks. Cybersecurity professionals work to develop and implement measures to prevent, detect, and respond to such attacks.

Overall, IT is an incredibly vast and diverse field, and there is always something new to learn and discover. As a first-year student, I am excited to continue exploring this field and seeing where it takes me. If you are interested in technology, I encourage you to do the same!
